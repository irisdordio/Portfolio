          Creativity isn’t tidy. Good work shouldn’t be either. We have traded
          depth for deliverables, we have traded process for polish, we have
          traded quiet practice for algorithmic performance. This space refuses
          that exchange. Keep the friction. Honor the drafts. Honor the dead
          ends and honor the unrendered hours that shape what lasts. This isn’t
          a showcase of polished outcomes but — a selected collection of
          finished projects, each carrying the quiet evidence of its making:
          iteration, doubt, and deliberate revision. If you still value the
          draft over the feed, stay.
        

      


      

        

          
          
          
          
          
          
          
          
          
          
          
          
          
          
        


        
        

          

            

              OOLOO is a technology consultancy that operates across complex
              digital ecosystems. The challenge was to translate that complexity
              into a clean, scalable visual language without leaning on typical
              tech clichés. Working at Studio Formato, I helped build a modular
              identity system grounded in typographic discipline and spatial
              clarity. My work spanned logo development, core visual guidelines,
              website UX, and institutional materials designed to perform
              consistently across digital, print, and presentation contexts. The
              result is a flexible, precision-driven brand that communicates
              intelligence through restraint and structure.
            

            

              
              
              
            

          

          

            

              
              
            

            
            

              
              
              
            

            
            

              
              
              
            

            
            

              
              
            

          

        


        
        

          

            

              Burn Ghost is a Web3 gaming platform operating at the intersection
              of digital culture and community. The challenge was to build a
              narrative-driven visual system flexible enough to support campaign
              launches, collectibles, and activations without losing coherence.
              Working collaboratively, I developed the campaign identity, core
              graphic assets, and the Genesis Pass collectible system, spanning
              posters, digital banners, launch visuals, and presentation
              materials. The visual language balances expressive energy with
              structural control, dynamic enough for gaming culture yet
              disciplined enough to scale. The Genesis Pass launch generated
              strong community engagement, proving the system's adaptability in
              a fast-moving landscape.
            

            

              
              
              
            

          

          

            

              
              
              
              

              
              
              
              
            

            
            

              
              
              
              
              
              
              
              
              
              
            

          

        


        
        

          

            

              Novas Novas Cartas Portuguesas is a bilingual exhibition catalogue
              developed for Galeria Quadrum, Lisbon, curated by Tobi Maier to
              mark the 50th anniversary of the landmark 1972 work. The challenge
              was to construct an editorial system capable of holding critical
              essays, archival material, and contemporary contributions within a
              single coherent framework. Invited to design the publication and
              contribute my own essay, “On The Fetish For Nuns,” I built the
              typographic structure, defined the grid logic, and managed
              bilingual pacing and print production. The layout prioritizes
              spatial rhythm and typographic control over visual dominance,
              allowing complex discourse to unfold with clarity. The result is a
              disciplined editorial object that balances cultural sensitivity
              with rigorous design precision.
            

            

              
              
              
            

          

          

            

              
              
            

            
            

              
              
            

            
            

              
              
              
            

            
          

        


        
        

          

            

              Festival Terras Sem Sombra is a cultural festival exploring the
              intersection of music, heritage, and landscape. The challenge was
              to define a contemporary visual identity that respects its
              historical context without relying on decorative heritage
              references. Co-developed with designer Sílvia Prudêncio, I led the
              conceptual direction, logo architecture, and typographic
              system—applied across digital communication formats for varied
              programming. The identity emphasizes spatial rhythm and
              compositional balance, creating a framework that evolves across
              editions while maintaining recognisability. The result establishes
              a culturally sensitive, structurally precise foundation built for
              longevity.
            

            

              
              
              
            

          

          

            

              
              
              
            

            
            

              
            

            
          

        

      


      

        

          
          

            

              I’m Íris Dórdio, a motion and visual designer based in Portugal. I
              build visual identity systems and motion-led assets that move from
              concept to final production without losing their structural
              integrity. My practice bridges creative strategy and technical
              execution: I blend design and motion to ensure every brand element
              scales cleanly across digital touchpoints. I’m drawn to projects
              where spatial thinking and intentional animation strengthen
              cohesive brand ecosystems. Open to studio, agency, and freelance
              collaborations.
